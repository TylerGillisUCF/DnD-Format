# THE SIX STATS & MODIFIERS

### Understanding the Stats

In D&D, characters have six core abilities that determine what they're good at. Here's what each stat represents:

# STRENGTH (STR)

## ‚öîÔ∏è What it does: Physical power, melee combat, lifting/breaking things, intimidation through presence

When to use: Pushing boulders, arm wrestling, melee attacks, forcing doors, climbing with brute force

### Character focus: Warriors, barbarians, anyone who solves problems with muscles

# DEXTERITY (DEX)

### What it does: Agility, reflexes, precision, stealth, ranged attacks, acrobatics

### When to use: Dodging, sneaking, pickpocketing, archery, balancing, quick reactions

### Character focus: Rogues, rangers, anyone who relies on speed and finesse

# CONSTITUTION (CON)

### What it does: Endurance, stamina, resisting effects, toughness

### When to use: Resisting poison, enduring harsh conditions, drinking contests, staying conscious

### Character focus: Anyone who needs to be tough and resilient

# INTELLIGENCE (INT)

### What it does: Logic, memory, reasoning, investigation, arcane knowledge

## ‚öîÔ∏è When to use: Solving puzzles, recalling lore, understanding magic, spotting patterns, deduction

### Character focus: Wizards, scholars, detectives, strategic thinkers

# WISDOM (WIS)

### What it does: Perception, insight, intuition, survival instincts, reading people/situations

### When to use: Spotting traps, sensing danger, understanding motives, tracking, medicine

### Character focus: Clerics, druids, anyone who trusts their gut

# CHARISMA (CHA)

### What it does: Force of personality, persuasion, deception, performance, leadership

### When to use: Negotiating, lying, inspiring, intimidating through personality, entertaining

### Character focus: Bards, leaders, diplomats, anyone who talks their way through problems

# MODIFIER CALCULATION

The number of points in a stat determines your MODIFIER - the bonus (or penalty) you add to dice rolls.

# HOW IT WORKS

When you roll a die for something related to a stat, you ADD the modifier to your roll.

# HOW TO ROLL

### STEP 1: DM announces what's being tested

"This is a Strength check!"

"Roll for Dexterity!"

"This requires Intelligence!"

### STEP 2: DM tells player which die to roll

"Roll a d10!"

"Give me a d20!"

"Roll d6!"

### STEP 3: Player rolls the die and announces result

"I got a 12!"

### STEP 4: Player adds their relevant stat modifier

"My Strength modifier is +2, so that's 14 total!"

### STEP 5: DM compares to target number (DC = Difficulty Class)

Easy tasks: DC 6-8

Medium tasks: DC 9-11

Hard tasks: DC 12-14

Very Hard tasks: DC 15-17

Nearly Impossible: DC 18+

### STEP 6: DM describes the outcome

### Success: "You accomplish the task!"

### Failure: "It doesn't work, but here's what happens instead..."

[DM TIP: You don't always have to reveal the DC beforehand. Sometimes it's more dramatic to let them roll and then reveal if they succeeded!]

### NATURAL 20s AND NATURAL 1s

### NATURAL 20 (Critical Success)

When the die itself shows 20 (before adding modifiers)

Automatic success, often with bonus effects!

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Player gains +1 coin as a reward

### CELEBRATE THIS! High-fives, cheers, excitement!

Example: "Natural 20! Not only do you pick the lock, you do it so smoothly that you find a hidden compartment with treasure inside! Plus you gain 1 coin!"

### NATURAL 1 (Critical Failure)

When the die itself shows 1 (before adding modifiers)

Automatic failure, often with funny consequences

Player loses 1 coin (EXCEPT in combat - no coin loss in fights!)

### LAUGH TOGETHER! These make the best stories

Example: "Natural 1! You try to climb the wall but your hand slips, and you slide back down, landing in a mud puddle. Everyone nearby tries not to laugh. You lose 1 coin as you fish it out of the mud!"

[DM TIP: Make Natural 1s funny, not punishing. The coin loss is enough - make the narrative outcome amusing, embarrassing, or slapstick, never truly harmful!]

## ‚öîÔ∏è COMBAT MECHANICS

Combat in this campaign is streamlined and exciting - no tracking roll outcomes, armor class, or roll penalties. Instead, combat is resolved through opposed rolls and best-of-X matches!

## ‚öîÔ∏è COMBAT STRUCTURE

## ‚öîÔ∏è BASIC COMBAT FORMAT

Hero rolls vs. Enemy rolls

Highest total wins that exchange

First to win X exchanges wins the combat

X varies by encounter (best of 3, best of 5, etc.)

## ‚öîÔ∏è NO COIN LOSS IN COMBAT

Even if you roll a Natural 1 in combat, you don't lose a coin

Combat is challenging enough without economic penalties!

This encourages brave, heroic action

## ‚öîÔ∏è Example Combat

> **DM NOTE:** DM: "The bandit challenges you to a duel! Best of 3 exchanges - first to win 2 wins the fight! Roll d10 plus Strength!"

### Exchange 1: Hero 14, Bandit 11 ‚Üí Hero wins (1-0)

### Exchange 2: Hero 9, Bandit 16 ‚Üí Bandit wins (1-1)

### Exchange 3: Hero 18, Bandit 12 ‚Üí Hero wins (2-1) Victory!

## ‚öîÔ∏è COMBAT VARIATIONS

This campaign includes many types of combat to keep things fresh:

# DIRECT DUEL

Hero vs. single opponent

Best of 3 or best of 5

Strength, Dexterity, or other relevant stat

## ‚öîÔ∏è SKILL CHALLENGE

Navigate hazardous environment

Roll to avoid obstacles/dangers

First to 3 successes wins

# TIMED RACE

Outrun or outpace opponent

Series of Dexterity or Constitution rolls

Highest cumulative total wins

## ‚öîÔ∏è BATTLE OF WITS

Social combat

Charisma or Intelligence rolls

Outsmart or outargue opponent

# GAUNTLET

Face multiple weaker opponents in sequence

Win each exchange to progress

Stamina challenge (Constitution)

# RIDDLE DUEL

Answer challenges correctly

Intelligence or Wisdom

First to 3 correct answers wins

[DM TIP: Describe combat vividly! When players win an exchange, describe their cool move. When enemies win, describe the counterattack. Make every roll feel like an action scene!]

## ‚öîÔ∏è WHAT HAPPENS IF YOU LOSE COMBAT?

# REMEMBER: NO ONE DIES!

Instead, losing combat results in:

# FUNNY OUTCOMES

You slip in mud and the enemy laughs at you

You're disarmed and have to retrieve your weapon

You surrender and are briefly captured (but escape)

# NARRATIVE SETBACKS

You're delayed and have to take a longer route

You lose your chance to get bonus information

The opponent escapes with something minor

# ALTERNATE SUCCESS

You lose the fight but win their respect

You're beaten but find another solution

Someone else intervenes to help

## ‚öîÔ∏è IMPORTANT: Even in "defeat," the story continues forward. No fight is a dead end.

[DM TIP: If a player is having terrible luck with rolls and losing repeatedly, let them find a creative solution that bypasses traditional combat. "You can't beat them in a fight... but maybe you could trick them instead?"]
